Under the rank-1 matrix model selected by 5-fold cross-validation for the covariate \log_{10}(EC)-\log_{10}(EO), the proposed projection-based inference indicates that the age association is not diffuse over the full ROI-by-band matrix. The global loading is not significantly different from zero, whereas several prespecified structured summaries are. At the ROI-slice level, the anterior-midline loading is significantly positive (\hat\delta=0.562, 95\% CI [0.169, 0.956]) and the central-right loading is significantly negative (\hat\delta=-0.529, 95\% CI [-0.979, -0.080]). At the band-slice level, only the alpha2 loading is significantly positive (\hat\delta=0.715, 95\% CI [0.243, 1.187]). Among single entries, six loadings have confidence intervals excluding zero, with the strongest signals concentrated in the theta and alpha2 bands. Thus, under the fitted low-rank log-contrast model, the data provide evidence for localized spatial and spectral structure rather than a uniform matrix-wide shift. This illustrates the inferential value of the proposed method: the low-rank fit is not only predictive, but also supports uncertainty quantification for prespecified ROI and band summaries that are scientifically interpretable.
